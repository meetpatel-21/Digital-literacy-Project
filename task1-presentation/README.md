# Project Overview

For this project, I used Canva as my primary digital tool to create a visually engaging infographic. Canva proved to be highly effective due to its user-friendly drag-and-drop interface, extensive library of templates, and versatile design elements, which allowed me to structure information clearly without requiring advanced graphic design skills.

My design visually organizes the five key digital literacy topics: core skills, useful tools, safe internet practices, professional presence, and privacy. I used a cohesive color scheme, icons, and distinct sections to make the content easy to navigate and visually appealing. The layout guides the viewer through each concept logically, emphasizing clarity and readability.

One aspect I found both interesting and challenging was balancing visual appeal with information density. While Canva offered many creative options, it was difficult to avoid overcrowding the design while ensuring all essential points were included. This process taught me the importance of minimalist design in effective communication—learning to prioritize key messages without sacrificing aesthetics was a valuable takeaway.
