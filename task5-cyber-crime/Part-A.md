# Case Study: The Silent Campaign
## The Crime
This case involves cyber harassment and impersonation—a “catfishing” scheme used to isolate a victim, violating anti-bullying statutes and university conduct codes.

## How It Happens
The Setup:  “Jordan” loses a student election to “Alex.” Motivated by jealousy, Jordan creates a fake social media account using a stolen photo.

The Lure:  Posing as this fake persona, Jordan romantically pursues Alex for weeks, extracting private thoughts and embarrassing photos.

The Attack:  Jordan screenshots the conversations and shares them in a large group chat, mocking Alex and adding false accusations.

Amplification: Jordan uses multiple fake accounts to pile on harassment, making it appear like a mob attack.

## Target
The target is often a high-achieving individual perceived as a rival. Alex was targeted due to jealousy over a leadership position.

## Consequences
Alex suffered severe anxiety and transferred schools. The university traced the fake accounts to Jordan, resulting in expulsion and a permanent disciplinary record. Jordan also faced potential civil liability for defamation and emotional distress.

## Conclusion
This case reveals that cyberbullying carries severe consequences beyond the screen. Jordan’s actions led to expulsion, a permanent disciplinary record, and potential legal liability. Alex suffered emotional distress and had to transfer schools.

## Prevention
Use privacy settings, never share sensitive content, report abuse immediately, and support victims. Bystanders: speak up—silence enables harm.
